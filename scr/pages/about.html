About Me

Who does this website belong to?

Willem Swanepoel or Will Swan for short, is a young and hopeful software developer currently living in Newcastle, Australia where he discovered a passion for programming through the language of Python and he never really forgot. Suffering from undiagnosed ADHD he found improving in the field difficult as he was subject to the whims of his condition.
However, due to his father getting diagnosed, it has come to light that Will Swan may aswell be suffering from ADHD. Since getting diagnosed and joining Coder Academy to teach him the ropes of the professional coding environment, his passion is finally being fulfilled.

Some facts about Will Swan.

  • I have been coding on and off since I was 15, having been introduced to it in highschool.
  • Some notible items I've done in Python before lossing the files was a game using the pygame module and producing a Discord Bot to play music in the voice chat function.
  • Other hobbies of mine include playing video games, I enjoy the technical elements being drawn to Redstone Engineering in games like Minecraft, or prefering roles like Sniper or Spy in competive First Person Shooters like TF2 and Halo.
  • While not medicated I am drawn to new things and will often dedicate upwards of 10 hours a day towards something if my ADHD chooses to hyper fixate on it. This however has put a strain on my passion for coding and since getting medicated has allowed me to continue this passion in a much healthier manner without lossing interest after the 2 week period.

What are Will Swan's skills?

  • Problem Solving is one of my most defining skills, comboing well with my ADHD, allows for divergent thinking of coming up with solutions not often though of by neurotypical minds
  • Despite my condition I have always been Organised everything always has a place, and I rarely loose items when they are where they belong.
  • Creativity is another skill I possess, though not creative in an artistic form, my creativity comes out in my solutions and approach to problems or simply in the ideas I have when making suggestions
  • ADHD isn't much a skill than a condition, however through learning to manage and live with it, even while undiagnosed, parts of it have become skills, as demonstrated above, I am dedicated and self motivated to get stuff done in a timely manner, often within the first 2 weeks of being givin the task, my attention to detail is often higher than most as small imperfections bother me, and its become a challange to learn to let things be.
